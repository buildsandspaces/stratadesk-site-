The problem

Writing up an AGM is the part of the job nobody got into property management for.

You sit through the meeting. Then you sit down again — that evening, or the next morning before everything else — to turn scrawled notes or a recording into something formal enough to send to every owner and hold up if anyone ever disputes a decision.

It's slow, it's repetitive across every scheme you manage, and it's the kind of writing where getting the structure right matters more than getting it written fast.
